A - Insights
1 - Study time doesn't change marks much: The correlation is -0.04, which is almost zero. This means studying more hours didn't always lead to higher marks in this data.
2 - Too much study might be bad: Students who studied more than 10 hours had the lowest average marks (44.7). This could mean they are getting tired or "burnt out."
3 - The "Best" study range: Students studying between 6 to 10 hours got the best average marks (69.6). This seems to be the perfect amount of time to study.
4 - Attendance has a small impact: Average marks for "Satisfactory" and "Excellent" students are very similar (about 69 marks). This shows that higher attendance didn't significantly change the average score.
5 - High passing rate: Most students in the dataset (1,086 out of 1,200) scored above 50 marks. This means the majority of students are performing at a passing level.

B - Patterns Observed
1 - The 10-Hour Drop: Marks are good for low and medium study hours, but they drop a lot once a student crosses 10 hours.
2 - Steady Performance: Average marks stay nearly the same across all attendance categories, showing a very stable trend.
